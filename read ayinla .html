AYINLA

    Àyìnlá (Male)
  • Meaning: A child meant to be praised, disciplined, and cherished; often associated with musicianship or a strong character.
  • Yoruba Oríkì
  • Àyìnlá, Ọmọ Ọmọwúrà, àdàgbà t'ó lé'pa owó.
  • Àyìnlá, Arúko-jẹ-gbàgbé.
  • Ẹni tí a yìn, tí ó fẹ́ kí a kà á sí Ọ̀gá.
  • Àyìnlá ọkùnrin ìkà, ọkùnrin ogun.
  • Bí a bá pè'jà á jà, bí a bá pè'ṣẹ́ á ṣiṣẹ́.
  • Ẹni tí inú rẹ̀ kò bá dùn sí, á yọ̀ rẹ́rẹ́.
  • Ọmọ àgbẹ̀dẹ́ gbẹ́lú.
  • Ẹyẹ onírun funfun lóko Ìdófẹ́.

    • English Meaning:

  • Ayinla, child of Omowura (a wealthy trader lineage), the elder of commerce.
  • Ayinla, one who eats what was sown and forgets (can imply forgiveness).
  • The one whom we praise, who wants us to consider him the boss.
  • Ayinla the formidable man, a man of war.
  • If we call for a fight, he fights; if we call for work, he works.
  • The one whose happiness is not full will still smile charmingly.
  • Child of the blacksmith who carves the city.
  • The bird with white feathers in the forest of Idofe (a specific reference).

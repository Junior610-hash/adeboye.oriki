AYINDE

  • Meaning: One who arrives when praised. Praising him brings out his best qualities.
  • Yoruba Oríkì
  • Àyìndé, ọmọ ọba, ọmọ Ọ̀wọ́.
  • Àyìndé, Arímọ́ni-wéwẹ́.
  • Ẹni tí orí rẹ̀ ti tó.
  • Ọmọ a-p'ẹran-p'ẹran-kò-f'ọrùn-jẹ.
  • Sọ̀rọ̀ rere sí i, inú rẹ̀ a dùn.
  • Àyìndé, Ọmọ àjà-b'orí-yọ.
  • Arẹmu-dáadáa, oní-dídùn-yèyé.

    • English Meaning:

  • Ayinde, child of the king, child from Owo (lineage).
  • Ayinde who owns many small horses (wealthy/powerful).
  • The one whose destiny is sufficient/fulfilled.
  • Child who kills animals but does not eat the neck (a traditional taboo or practice of the lineage).
  • Speak well of him, and he will be happy.
  • Ayinde, child who fights and emerges with his head (survives great challenges).
  • The proper first-born, owner of a sweetness that is motherly.
  • Child of Olugbon-Orantun (lineage name).

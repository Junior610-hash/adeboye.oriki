ADISA

Àdísà, the clear, powerful, and decisive one. Yoruba (Extensive):

  • Àdísà Ọlúgbọ́n, Ọmọ Àsàmú.
  • Ọmọ amá bẹ̀rẹ̀ gunyan ká dó gberigbe m'ámọ̀.
  • Àdísà, ọkùnrin inú ogun, ẹkùn ti ń gbọ́n nínú ìgbọ́n.
  • Ọmọ Olówá, Ọmọ Ẹkùn, tíí r'ẹsẹ̀ ìmọ̀ kúnlẹ̀.
  • Àdísà, kò sí ohun tó lè di ọ́ mọ́lẹ̀, Ọmọ ọba tó dé láti Ìbàdàn.
  • Ẹ kù díẹ̀ kẹ́ dónà, ẹ domi ọmọ.
  • Àdísà, oníwà-pè̩lú, onírínú-dídùn.
  • B'ọ́ba fé rí 'wà-pè̩lú, ojú Àdísà lọ wá.
  • Ọmọ abénu gbọnyín gbọnyín b'àdó.
  • Bí a bá fẹ́ rí aláṣẹ àgbà, ojú Àdísà la wo.

English Meaning:

  • Adisa the highly perceptive/wise one, child of Asamu.
  • Child who starts by pounding yam and reaches the top with a clear character/head.
  • Adisa, a man of war/courage, a wise leopard in the forest of wisdom.
  • Child of the ruler, child of the leopard, who sees the foundation of knowledge and kneels before it (humble/wise).
  • Adisa, nothing can hold you captive, child of the king who arrived from Ibadan (lineage link).
  • You narrowly missed the road (to the afterlife), you became the child of water (survived a close call).
  • Adisa, the owner of a calm and pleasant character.
  • If a king wants to see a calm character, he should look at Adisa's face.
  • Child with a small mouth like a gourd (implies precise/valuable speech).
  • If we want to see a great authority figure, we look at Adisa's face.

AKANNI

    . Àkànní, the rare, unique, and purposeful one. Yoruba (Extensive):
  • Àkànní, Ọmọ ọlọ́jọ́ kún.
  • Àkànní, Ọmọ a-b'ọ́rẹ́-kọ́-gbàgbé.
  • Ọkùnrin dúdú ó yọ rẹ́rẹ́, aláṣọ funfun, onítẹ̀ẹ́gùn mẹ́wàá.
  • Ọmọ àgbẹ̀dẹ́ gbẹ́lú, ọmọ agínná t'ojú ewé gbóná.
  • Àkànní, ọ̀kan ṣoṣo, onígun méjìlá, àkànṣe èèyàn.
  • Ẹni tí a kò lè rí lójú ogun, nítorí pé ó ti rí ìjà tán.
  • Ọmọ dáńgúdà-dáńgúdà l'ẹ́sẹ̀ tí ń yan.
  • Àkànní, Arẹmu dáadáa, Ọmọ Olófin àti Ọlọ́run.
  • Bí kò rí mi, kò le jẹun, bí mo bá rí i, inú mi a dùn.
  • Ọmọ tí gbogbo ayé ń fẹ́ rí lójú ogun àti lójú àlàáfíà.
  • Àkànní, abẹ́lẹ́ṣẹ̀-mọ́rín.

    • English Meaning:

  • Akanni, the child who completes the day/fills the destiny.
  • Akanni, child who gives gifts and doesn't forget the recipient (is reliable/consistent).
  • Dark-complexioned man who smiles charmingly, owner of white cloth (purity/Obatala lineage), owner of ten stable, powerful legs.
  • Child of the blacksmith who carves the city, child of the flame that burns hot from a mere leaf (inherent power).
  • Akanni, the only one of his kind, a twelve-cornered person (complex/complete), a uniquely made human.
  • The one who cannot be found during war because he has ended the fight already (proactive).
  • Child with firm and steady steps as he walks.
  • Akanni, the proper first-born, child of the owner of the law (tradition) and the owner of the heavens (God).
  • If he doesn't see me, he can't eat; if I see him, I am happy (mutual importance).
  • The child whom everyone wants to see both during war and during peace.
  • Akanni, the one with clean/smooth feet.

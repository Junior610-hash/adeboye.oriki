IGE ADUBI

Ìgè Adúbí Ìgè Adúbí, the strong, exceptional one whose unusual arrival destined him for fortune. Yoruba (Extensive):

  • Ìgè Adúbí, Àgbo-lẹnu-bu-àgogo.
  • Ọmọ onígbagbára ìràwọ̀, tí ń dán mọ́lẹ̀ nínú òkùnkùn.
  • Bí ìyá lè kú, kò kú. Bí bàbá lè lọ, kò ní lọ.
  • Ohun Ìgè Adúbí yóò jẹ kò ní í wọ́n-ọ́n; ọbẹ̀ tí yóò fi jẹun kò ní tán.
  • Ìgè Adúbí kò r'ojú ìyá, ojú bàbá l'Adúbí ń rò.
  • Ẹni b'Égè Adúbí níṣe, ara rẹ̀ ló bẹ.
  • Àdùbí, ohun bí eegun, alágbára púpọ̀.
  • Ab'igi Owó s'ọ́kọ bí ẹdùn, ajẹ́'yọ-má-gbà-lọ́wọ́-ẹni.
  • Ọmọ ẹni tí kò lè ṣàì jẹ olówó nítorí orúkọ rẹ̀.
  • A-rọ̀yé-jẹun, a-jẹ-jẹ-gbà-lọ́wọ́-olóko-tán.

English Meaning:

Ige Adubi, whose voice resonates like a bell (loud, important). Child of the one who owns countless stars, who shines brightly in the darkness. If the mother could have died (from the birth), she didn't. If father could leave (due to stress), he won't. Whatever Ige Adubi will eat, he will never lack; the soup he eats with will never finish. Ige Adubi didn't face his mother's face (at birth), he faced his father's face (unusual arrival). The person who tasks Ige Adubi is tasking himself (implies high capability). Adubi, a strong person like a bone, very powerful. One who grows money trees in the farm like a monkey (implies wealth), one who eats to satisfaction without taking from others. Child of the one who must be wealthy because of his name/destiny. One who gets food after an argument/struggle, one who eats everything from the farmer's hand. A "complete" oríkì is an extensive, performative oral poem that can go on for a long time, often specific to an individual's deep ancestral lineage (oríkì ìdílé).

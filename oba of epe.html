ORÍKÌ OBA OF EPE — Olu / Oloja of Epe

  • Olu Epe, Ọba ọmọ Erinmi
  • Ọba tí ó kó omi sílẹ̀ kí omi tó wà
  • Ọmọ ará ilú tí omi bò lọ́wọ́
  • A kì í rìn s’Epe kí omi má bà wa lọ́wọ́
  • Omo ọlọ́kò tí ń tọ́ ẹja lọ́na
  • Ọba tí a bí ní omi tí ilẹ̀ gbà
  • Ọmọ a-ko-jẹ-kí ẹ̀fúùrù gbọ́ orúkọ rẹ̀
  • Adúgbò ń gbelè nírú rẹ̀ bá kú
  • Oba tí ilẹ̀ àti omi ń bẹ̀rù rẹ̀
  • Ẹni tí ẹja ń yọ̀ sí
  • Omo tí orí rẹ̀ wà níbẹ̀ kí orí omi tó mọ̀
  • A rìn-ń-jọba ní etí omi
  • Ọba tí a kò lè kó silè
  • Olu Epe tí gbogbo ilẹ̀ ń bẹ̀rù
  • Kí ọlá rẹ̀ ó pé
  • Kí Epe ó ní ire àti ìbùkún omi àti ilẹ̀ Ase.

    • Meaning in English — Oba of Epe

  • Olu Epe, child of Erinmi
  • A royal who commands water before it flows
  • Born of a land protected by the sea
  • No one enters Epe without meeting water’s welcome
  • Child of fishermen, leader of the deep
  • A king birthed by water yet grounded by land
  • One whose name whispers power
  • The neighborhood mourns when such royalty departs
  • A ruler feared by both land and ocean
  • The fish rejoice at his presence
  • The winds dare not scatter his legacy
  • His crown existed before even the sea gained depth
  • A monarch ruling by the waterside
  • One who cannot be cast down
  • The Olu of Epe whom all lands respect
  • May Epe prosper with blessings of both land and water Ase.

ADUKE

  • Meaning: The child for whom people will "fight over the privilege to care for her"; beloved and desirable.
  • Yoruba Oríkì
  • Àdùké, Ọmọ ọba àdó, Aríké-dáadáa.
  • Àdùké, Olùfẹ́ gbogbo ayé.
  • Ọmọ a-b'ẹnu-pọ́n-mọ́, a-b'ẹ̀ṣẹ̀-tẹ̀-yọ̀yọ̀.
  • Àdùké, ìgbà tó yẹ ni yóò tó jẹun.
  • Àdùké, ohun bí eegun.
  • Ọmọ Olófin àti Ọlọ́run.
  • Ọmọ ewé ti ń gbóná lójú ẹran.
  • Ọmọ Olúgbọ́n-Ọ̀rántú

    • English Meaning:

  • Aduke, child of the King of Ado (lineage), a good person to pamper on sight.
  • Aduke, the beloved of all the world.
  • Child with a comforting mouth, and feet that step gracefully.
  • Aduke, she will only eat when it is proper/time (disciplined).
  • Aduke, a strong person like a bone.
  • Child of the owner of the law (tradition) and the owner of the heavens (God).
  • Child of the leaf that burns hot on the face of meat (inherent power and effect).
  • Child of Olugbon-Orantun (lineage name)

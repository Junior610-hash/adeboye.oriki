ARIKE

    Àríkẹ́ (Arike)
  • Meaning: Meant to be cherished/spoiled on sight.
  • Oriki (Yoruba):
  • Àríkẹ́, àríkẹ́ ajé,
  • Ọmọ a rẹ́gì bèrè ìwọ̀fà,
  • A jí pẹ́lẹ́ pẹ́lẹ́ bí iye ẹyẹ,
  • Ọmọ olowo, omo olokun,
  • Ajíbá níbi yíyan ìyán.

    • Meaning (English):

  • Arike, the one found and pampered by wealth,
  • Child of the one who cuts a piece of cloth to ask for a servant,
  • The one who wakes up as gently as a bird's feather,
  • Child of the wealthy, child of the ocean owner (wealthy person),
  • The one who wakes up to the pounding of yam.
  • Àríkẹ́ (Arike)
  • Meaning: One who is to be cared for or pampered upon sight.
  • Oríkì Snippets (Yoruba & English):
  • Àríkẹ́, àgbéríyìí.
  • Arike, the one whose head (destiny) brings honor/respect.
  • Ọmọ bíńtí, oníwọ̀sùn (often Ọmọ bíńtí, oníwọ̀sùn ofẹ́ - soft brocade).
  • The small child, owner of soft, high-quality cloth/brocade.
  • Àríkẹ́, àyànmọ́.
  • Arike, the one specifically chosen by destiny.
  • Ọmọ afọ̀rànkẹ̀ṣẹ́ bọni.
  • Child of the one who treats others with delicate care, like a ripe cashew fruit.

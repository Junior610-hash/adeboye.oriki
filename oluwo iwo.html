Oriki of the Oluwo of Iwo

    Orílẹ̀ (Lineage/Town Praise)
  • Oluwo ti Iwo. The King of Iwo.
  • Iwo, ilu amure. Iwo, the city of the armed (or the girded ones).
  • Iwo, ilu igba lumole. Iwo, the city where two hundred people light up the ground (signifying a powerful and numerous population).
  • Omo Oniseru, omo Onisenu. Child of the owner of the sacred pot, child of the owner of the sacred mortar (referencing ancient traditional items of power).
  • Eni omo erin si n sin ni Iwo. The place where the child of the elephant still serves as attendants (indicating strength and majesty).
  • T'elegan ba n sunkun, Iwo a ma yo. If the envious person is crying, Iwo people rejoice (a bold declaration of their distinct identity and resilience).

    • Oriki Àbísọ / Àjùmọ̀ṣun (Personal/Hereditary Praise)

  • Oba Abdulrasheed Adewale Akanbi. (The King's full name: Adewale means "the crown comes home"; Akanbi means "one who is deliberately chosen/born").
  • Telu l. Telu the First (His reign name, implying "possessor of the world").
  • Oba igbalumole. The king who lights up the ground (a modern take on the town's original oriki, signifying his impactful reign).
  • Oluwo, Ade-ogba. Oluwo, the crown that embraces/holds many.
  • Aji lowo sere. One who wakes up in the morning to play with money/wealth (implying immense wealth and ease).
  • Omo oloore, omo oloola. Child of a kind person, child of a person with good character/honor.
  • Omo aforan dade. The child who takes a case/issue and wears it as a crown (implying wisdom in judgement and authority).

    • Oriki Àìbátan (Praise for Achievements and Royal Essence)

  • Oluwo ti o n gb'oluwo l'owo eni. The king who takes kingship/power from someone's hand (a powerful phrase implying his decisive and authoritative nature).
  • Emi ni Telu, emi ni ka bi ye si. I am Telu, I am the one to whom you pay homage.
  • Gbogbo eni ti n ba mi ja, won o gbodo ri mi l'oju. All those who fight with me must never see my face (a statement of his untouchable power and protection).
  • Oba alagbara, Oba atayese igbalumole. The powerful king, the king who improves the world, the one who lights up the ground.
  • Oba who makes the final judgment (lit. kills the case), and speaks only the truth.
  • Akanbi omo Adeogun. Akanbi, the child of the crown of war/strength.

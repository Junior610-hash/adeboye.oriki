AJANI

    . Àjàní, the cherished one, the warrior. Yoruba (Extensive):
  • Àjàní, Òkín-Ọ̀run.
  • Àjàní, Alàjàní ọ̀gbọ́n.
  • Ọmọ Ẹkùn, ọmọ Olóró Erin.
  • Ọmọ bí kò le wà á wá, kò le wà á mú.
  • Ohun rere ṣ'ọ̀wọ́, nítorí náà la bá ti jà kí á tó ní ọ́.
  • Àjàní, ọkùnrin ìkà, ọkùnrin ogun.
  • Bí a bá pè'jà, á jà, bí a bá pè'ṣẹ́, á ṣiṣẹ́.
  • Àjàní, afí'yà-f'ẹnu yọ́.
  • Ọmọ Olówó-inú, ẹni tíí b'ọ́rẹ́-kọ́-gbàgbé.
  • Ọmọ Olówó kan nínú ìlú tí ń b'ọ́rẹ́ kọ́ ni rárá.
  • Bí ẹyẹ bá fẹ́ jẹ èso, á bẹ́gi; ẹni bá fẹ́ Àjàní á jà fún un.
  • A-lówó-lọ́jà-títí-tí-tí-tí, a-lọ́jà-lọ́jà-kò-bánigbá.
  • Ọmọ ewé tí ń gbóná lójú ẹran.

    • English Meaning:

  • Ajani, the majestic peacock of the heavens.
  • Ajani, the immensely wise one.
  • Child of the Leopard, child of the owner of the elephant's toxin (powerful and dangerous to rivals).
  • A child who cannot be accessed or taken easily (rare and valuable).
  • Good things are scarce, that is why we fought to have you.
  • Ajani, a formidable man, a man for war.
  • If we call for a fight, he fights; if we call for work, he works (versatile and ready).
  • Ajani, who makes people happy with his mouth after enduring pain (diplomatic/enduring).
  • Child of the generous heart, one who gives gifts and doesn't forget the recipient.
  • Child of a wealthy person in the town who gives gifts consistently.
  • If a bird wants to eat a fruit, it must first fly to the tree; whoever wants Ajani must fight for him.
  • Owner of money in the market forever, owner of a market that is busy but doesn't take others' wealth.
  • Child of the leaf that burns hot on the face of meat (quick-acting power)

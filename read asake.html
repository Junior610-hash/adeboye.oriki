ASAKE

    Àsàkẹ́ (Asake)
  • Meaning: Selected/Chosen to be cherished or spoiled.
  • Oriki (Yoruba):
  • Àsàkẹ́, ọmọ ògo,
  • Ọmọ ẹlẹ́gbẹ̀dẹ kan òṣùṣù,
  • Èyí tí ẹgbẹ̀dẹ gbé tiẹ̀ fúnni,
  • Ọmọ tí a sà fúnra ẹni jẹ.
  • Ayanfé Olóhùn.

    • Meaning (English):

  • Asake, child of glory,
  • Child of the one with a large cane/staff,
  • The one who presents their cane to others,
  • The child we selected for ourselves to cherish.
  • The chosen one of God.

  • Àṣàkẹ́ (Asake)
  • Meaning: One who is selected or picked to be cherished and pampered.
  • Oríkì Snippets (Yoruba & English):
  • Àṣàkẹ́, ọmọ ẹlẹ́gbẹ̀dẹ.
  • Asake, the child (descendant) of the wearer of the ẹgbẹ̀dẹ (a type of waist bead/adornment, symbolizing elegance and status).
  • Ọmọ abata, abata yọ́ kẹ̀tẹ̀kẹ̀tẹ̀.
  • Child of the swampy area (a common lineage reference), the swamp that yields softly underfoot.
  • Àṣàkẹ́, àyànfẹ́ Olóhun.
  • Asake, the chosen one of God.
